Essential Items to Prepare for a Hurricane




Hot Club Tracks Staff - October 8th, 2024


Hurricanes are powerful natural disasters that require thorough preparation to ensure the safety of you and your loved ones. Here is a list of 20 essential things you need to have on hand before a hurricane hits, including recommended products for some of the most important items.

1. Non-perishable Food

Stock up on canned goods, dried fruits, nuts, and other non-perishable items that do not require refrigeration. Make sure you have enough for at least three days for each person in your household.

2. Water

You will need at least 1 gallon of water per person per day for at least three days. This covers both drinking and sanitation purposes.

3. Battery-Powered or Hand-Crank Radio

Staying informed is critical during a hurricane. A reliable weather radio will keep you updated with emergency alerts and news. One excellent option is the Raynic Emergency Radio, which features a 5000mAh battery, solar and hand-crank charging, AM/FM/SW/NOAA alerts, a phone charger, flashlight, and SOS siren.

4. Flashlights and Extra Batteries

In the event of power outages, flashlights are essential for navigating safely in the dark. Consider the EVEREADY LED Flashlights (4-Pack), which come with AA batteries included and are designed for emergencies and camping.

5. First Aid Kit

A well-stocked first aid kit should include bandages, antiseptic wipes, adhesive tape, and any prescription medications that your family needs.

6. Personal Hygiene Items

Maintaining hygiene is important even during a disaster. The 48 Sets Basic Toiletry Kits include disposable toothbrushes, toothpaste, shampoo, combs, and soap â€“ all packaged for travel or emergency situations.

7. Important Documents

Keep important documents, such as ID, medical records, and insurance policies, in a waterproof container. Make copies if possible.

8. Cash

ATMs and card machines may not work during a power outage, so having cash on hand is vital.

9. Portable Chargers

Keep your phones and devices charged with portable power banks. For a more robust solution, the TB600 Portable Power Station offers 556Wh of backup power, with AC outlets and USB-C ports, ideal for long outages.

10. Multi-Purpose Tool

A versatile tool is crucial for handling quick repairs. The RAK Universal Socket Tool includes a socket wrench, screwdriver bits, and more, making it an excellent addition to your emergency kit.

11. Full Gas Tank

If you need to evacuate quickly, ensure your car's gas tank is full to avoid being stranded.

12. Emergency Contact List

Prepare a list of contacts, including family, friends, and local shelters. Having it printed is helpful in case your phone is out of power.

13. Evacuation Plan

Know the evacuation routes in your area and be familiar with local shelter locations. Plan how to move your pets and any family members with special needs.

14. Prescription Glasses/Contact Lenses

Have a backup pair of prescription glasses or contact lenses on hand, as replacing them during a disaster could be difficult.

15. Backup Power Source

Consider investing in a portable power generator for essential appliances. This ensures you can keep refrigerators, medical devices, or communication devices running.

16. Manual Can Opener

Many emergency food supplies come in cans, so a manual can opener is essential when the power is out.

17. Sturdy Shoes and Protective Clothing

Floodwaters, debris, and broken glass can pose serious hazards. Be sure to have durable shoes and clothing ready for these conditions.

18. Fire Extinguisher

A fire extinguisher should be readily accessible in case of a fire, especially when candles or portable stoves are being used.

19. Plastic Sheeting and Duct Tape

These can be used to seal windows, doors, and air vents to protect against wind and water damage.

20. Pets' Supplies

Don't forget about your pets! Pack enough food, water, and any medications your pets need to last at least three days.

By ensuring you have these essentials in place, you will be better equipped to handle the challenges that hurricanes bring. Proper preparation can make all the difference in keeping your family safe.
