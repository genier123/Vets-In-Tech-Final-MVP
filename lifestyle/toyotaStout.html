The New Toyota Stout: Compact Pickup with a Legacy




The Toyota Stout, a name that resonates with history and dependability, is making a comeback. Originally launched in 1964, the Stout was Toyota’s first pickup truck built to compete with American trucks. Known for its toughness, it was popular among farmers, construction workers, and other industries that demanded a strong and dependable vehicle.


Anticipated Design and Performance


The new Toyota Stout is expected to be a monocoque compact pickup truck. It’s speculated to use the TNGA-K platform and feature a design based on the Compact Cruiser EV Concept. The Stout is expected to adopt engines from the RAV4 and Highlander, potentially offering a hybrid trim.


Engine options might include the RAV4’s 2.5-liter Dynamic Force engine or the Highlander’s 2.4-liter turbo I4. These options could give the Stout an advantage in power and fuel economy compared to competitors like the Ford Maverick and Hyundai Santa Cruz. It’s also expected to have competitive payload and towing capacities.


Exciting Features and Options


The new Toyota Stout is anticipated to come with a host of exciting features:

  • 12.3" interactive touchscreen display
  • Apple CarPlay and Android Auto compatibility
  • Qi wireless charging pad
  • Head-up display and digital dashboard cluster
  • JBL surround sound audio system
  • Wi-Fi connectivity

These features aim to provide a comfortable and connected driving experience.


Pricing and Release Date

The new Toyota Stout is likely to start below $25,000 and may be revealed in mid-2024. However, Toyota has yet to confirm these details.

Conclusion

The return of the Toyota Stout is a significant event, demonstrating Toyota’s devotion to their legacy and their willingness to cater to a diverse spectrum of customers. With its anticipated features and performance, the new Toyota Stout is set to make a strong entry into the compact pickup truck class.
